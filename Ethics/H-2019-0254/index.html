Information Statement for the Research Project
Understanding basic cognition by categorising simple perceptual stimuli: Investigating the cause of perceptual choice

 

 

You are invited to participate in the research project identified above which is being conducted by Prof. Scott Brown, Dr. Ami Eidels and Dr Keith Nesbitt from the School of Psychology and the School of Information Technology at the University of Newcastle. The data collected will be analysed and may be used towards the PhD thesis of students from these schools.

 

Why is the research being done?

The purpose of the research is to examine performance under cognitive workload. We are interested in how people's behaviour changes as workload varies. This research is being funded by the Australian Research Council.

 

Who can participate in the research?

Anyone who has not previously participated in this experiment is able to participate.

 

What choice do you have?

Participation in this research is entirely your choice.  Only those people who give their informed consent will be included in the project.  Whether or not you decide to participate, your decision will not disadvantage you.

 

If you do decide to participate you may withdraw at any time during the experiment without giving a reason.

 

What would you be asked to do?

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to take part in a cognitive decision making task. You will complete the experiment using a computer, where you will be given instructions on how to complete the task. You will also be equipped with device that will occasionally display a light, and/or slight tactile stimulation similar to the vibration of a mobile phone. You may be asked to complete a practice phase to familiarise yourself with the task before beginning the actual experiment. During the task you will be presented with a series of simple stimuli on the screen, such as words or shapes, and will be asked to make simple decisions about them. Simultaneously, you will also be asked to press a button when the device displays a light, or presents a small tactile stimulus. In addition to recording the responses you make in the experiment, we will also ask for some demographic information and other related questions.

 

Will I be reimbursed for my participation?

SONA participants will receive 2 points credit per hour for their time and efforts. Other participants will receive a gift voucher (valid at many large stores and online sites) to the value of $15 for each hour of participation.

 

How much time will it take?

The experiment will take approximately 60 minutes to complete.

 

What are the risks and benefits of participating?

We cannot promise you any benefit from participating in this research.

 

How will your privacy be protected?

Should you chose to participate, your responses will be stored electronically for a period of 5 years. All data will be labelled with a random code number so throughout this time you will not be identifiable by your responses.

 

How will the information collected be used?

The data we collect from these experiments will contribute towards helping understanding cognitive psychological processes. In addition, these results may contribute towards research articles in scientific journals, with data in a de-identified form.

 

Individual participants will not be uniquely identified in any of the above publications.

 

If you wish to receive feedback about the results of the experiment, please email scott.brown@newcastle.edu.au.

 

What do you need to do to participate?

Please read this Information Statement and be sure you understand its contents before you consent to participate.  If there is anything you do not understand, or you have questions, contact the researcher. 

 

If you would like to participate, please inform the researcher.
